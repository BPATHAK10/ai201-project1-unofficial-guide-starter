Finding Housing Off Campus: What to Expect

Getting Started

UC Berkeley offers several resources for off-campus housing searches. Students should consult UC Berkeley Off-Campus (OCH) Rental Services for free support and listings exclusive to Berkeley students. The Berkeley Student Cooperative provides "housing at a lower cost for students at UC Berkeley and other Bay Area colleges and universities." Graduate students, international students, and those with basic needs concerns have specialized guides available.

Timeline for Searching

According to student experiences shared in the article:

Melissa's approach: Started exploring options in late fall semester, then intensified the search in March and April for fall housing. "It took a few weeks to find the right fit — my roommates and I signed our lease in late April to begin on June 2."

Preslee's recommendation: Begin searching 6-8 weeks before your intended move-in date. She signed her lease in July before her first fall semester.

Mahrosh's insight: If you start late, subleasing or co-op applications (typically available in spring) remain viable options.

Finding Roommates

Methods include meeting people in residence halls, posting on social media, or using the official Berkeley Discord's roommate channel. When seeking roommates, provide apartment photos, establish clear pricing, conduct video calls, and discuss lifestyle expectations upfront.

Search Considerations

Melissa recommends creating "a spreadsheet that listed out apartments" tracking cost, utilities, lease start dates, distance from campus, and transit access. Touring properties in person proved invaluable.

Preslee prioritized location and cost, seeking walkable distance and convenient amenities like meal plans and cleaning services.

Finding Your Apartment

Students successfully used platforms like Apartments.com, Zillow, and OCH-affiliated listing services. One student discovered housing through The Berkeley Group, while another accessed sorority housing options.

Lease, Utilities, and Tenant Rights

Key considerations include:
- Lease terms vary significantly regarding renters insurance requirements, utility coverage, cosigner needs, and subletting policies
- "Read the terms and conditions of your contract carefully" to identify hidden monthly fees
- Utilities may be included, separate, or at fixed rates depending on the property
- Student Legal Services provides resources on tenant rights, including a tip sheet on lease terminology
- The Berkeley Rent Board and California Tenant Guide offer additional renter protections
- The Housing Security Deposit Award Fund assists students unable to afford upfront security deposits
