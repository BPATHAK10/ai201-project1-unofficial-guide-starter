Off-Campus Housing Search Tips

Location Overviews

Before searching for a rental, familiarize yourself with Berkeley's neighborhoods:

Southside is the busy, student-oriented area adjacent to the south of campus. It features numerous on and off-campus student housing options, stores, and cafes on Telegraph Avenue and College Avenue.

Northside is quieter than Southside with apartments, co-ops, and family homes throughout hilly terrain.

Downtown begins a block west of campus around Shattuck Avenue, offering a mix of apartments and houses close to BART, restaurants, and west campus.

For more affordable options, consider nearby communities:
- North Oakland/Rockridge offers easy public transportation access, bike paths to campus, and a neighborhood feel
- Downtown Oakland provides dense urban living accessible via BART and bus stops
- Emeryville features shopping, entertainment, new apartment housing, and free shuttle buses to BART
- El Cerrito has two BART stations, affordable housing, small-town atmosphere, and convenient commuting

OCH: Start Here

UC Berkeley Off-Campus Housing Rental Services is the official search site for listings, sublets, and roommate connections.

Timeline for Housing Search: According to UC Berkeley Housing Officer Elaine Perkins-Smith: "Wait six to eight weeks before your move-in date to secure housing" and "not to secure housing until you start seeing the start date that the listers are interested in."

Screening Process: "OCH will look at the listing to make sure everything looks okay. If anything looks shaky, I will reach out to the lister to get more information."

Navigating the Site: Set two main parameters: maximum rent amount and whether you need furnished units. The officer notes: "Sharing rent will always be way cheaper than finding your own place."

Timing Advice: "The main question is how early should I secure housing. I always tell students they have more than enough time to apply for University housing and see what they get."

Contact OCH at 510-210-0650 or via email with questions.

Dorm-Style Apartments

Popular privately owned dorm-style options include:
- The Berk on College
- The Durant
- Hillside Village
- The Kittredge
- Metropolitan Student Housing
- The Standard
- The Berkeley Group

Research availability and rates for each. Schedule virtual or in-person tours before committing. The Berkeley Group operates multiple furnished, move-in ready properties with cleaning services, though management varies by location.

Other Search Sites

UC Berkeley Off-Campus Housing Facebook Page connects students seeking roommates, posting listings, or asking housing questions.

Zillow and Apartments.com are general housing search platforms that direct you to building websites. Always verify listings through OCH if concerned about authenticity.

General Tips Before Signing the Lease

Preparation measures:
- Have a cosigner, references, and landlord information ready
- Search the official UC Berkeley Discord for roommates
- Bring credit report, application, and deposit advance
- Clarify whether it's a sublease or rental, understand utilities
- Take photos and videos during visits
- Verify safety requirements like window locks and smoke detectors
- Budget for first month fees, deposits, moving supplies, and appliances
- Confirm your building meets City of Berkeley habitability requirements, including working gas and plumbing and intact doors and windows
- Map nearby grocery stores
- Consider Berkeley Dining meal plans (off-campus plans available)
- Create a spending plan through the Center for Financial Wellness
