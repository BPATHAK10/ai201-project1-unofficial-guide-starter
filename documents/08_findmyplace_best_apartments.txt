7 Best Apartments Near UC Berkeley (2026 Student Housing Guide)

Overview

Berkeley's housing market is competitive, with the citywide median rent at $2,870/month in 2026—roughly 50% above the national average. Most students secure housing through three main routes: Berkeley Student Cooperative (BSC), shared rentals in specific neighborhoods, or managed co-living services. Realistic per-person costs range from $750–$1,600/month.

1. FOUND Study Southside Berkeley — Best Fully Furnished Option
Location: 2201 Dwight Way (7-minute walk to campus)
Pricing: Starting around $850/month per room
Description: 2- and 4-bedroom furnished apartments featuring three rooftop decks with Bay views, 24/7 gym, laundry, and bike storage. All furniture and utilities included.
Pros: Move-in ready; no setup required; close to campus; premium amenities
Cons: Higher price point than traditional rentals; limited customization

2. Berkeley Student Cooperative (BSC) — Best for Budget-Conscious Students
Location: Seventeen co-ops near campus
Pricing: $750–$1,155/month (all-inclusive with food and utilities)
Description: Student-run cooperative housing with shared responsibilities. Options range from large houses (124+ residents) to apartment-scale units.
Pros: Lowest cost in Berkeley; includes meals and utilities; community-focused
Cons: Requires 5 hours weekly house work; application cycles fill quickly; year-long wait if missed

3. Tripalink Berkeley — Best Managed Co-Living Experience
Location: Multiple properties, including 1717 University Ave (Bosco)
Pricing: Starting around $1,628/month for full units (splits by occupancy)
Description: Professionally managed, furnished co-living with utilities included. Configurations range from 2- to 6-bedroom units.
Pros: Responsive management; furnished and utilities handled; ideal for international students
Cons: Premium pricing for convenience; less independence

4. Panoramic Berkeley — Best Modern Building on Telegraph
Location: 2539 Telegraph Ave (6 minutes from campus)
Pricing: $2,400–$5,900+/month depending on size and floor
Description: Contemporary building with nine-foot ceilings, oversized windows, and functional air conditioning. Studios to 4-bedrooms available.
Pros: Modern amenities; newer construction; excellent location
Cons: Expensive even by Berkeley standards; best suited for upper-level students with larger budgets

5. Channing Way Private Rentals — Best for Traditional Apartment Hunters
Location: Between Telegraph and Shattuck Avenue
Pricing: $1,200/month (private rooms in 4-bedrooms); $2,395/month (1-bedrooms)
Description: Older multi-unit buildings with variable pricing and negotiable terms for early signings. Less than 10-minute walk to campus.
Pros: Better value than co-living; landlord negotiation possible; authentic apartment experience
Cons: Requires direct research and phone outreach; older buildings; landlord variability

6. Durant Avenue Area — Best Balance of Campus Access and Commute Options
Location: Between Telegraph and Shattuck Avenue
Pricing: Approximately $1,550/month per bedroom in 4-bedroom units
Description: One block from Sather Gate with proximity to Downtown Berkeley BART for Bay Area commutes and internships.
Pros: Dual access (campus + public transit); flexible for varied student needs
Cons: Older building stock; limited modern updates

7. UC Berkeley Housing Apartments — Best for UC-Managed Peace of Mind
Location: Manville Apartments, Channing-Bowditch, and select Panoramic units
Pricing: Below private market rates (university-set annually)
Description: Directly managed by UC Berkeley with tenant protections and responsive maintenance. Limited availability.
Pros: Below-market pricing; strong tenant protections; university accountability
Cons: Extremely limited spots; firm application deadlines; less flexibility

Search Timeline Recommendation

Begin your search in October, one year before your August move-in date. Quality units typically sign between November and February. Starting your search in March means working with remaining inventory.
